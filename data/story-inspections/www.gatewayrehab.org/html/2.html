    Frequently Asked Questions

    What are the most common opioids?

    Some of the most common opioids include:

    • Codeine
    • Hydrocodone (Vicodin)
    • Morphine
    • Oxycodone (Oxycontin or Percocet)
    • Fentanyl

    What are the signs of opioid abuse in teens?

    • Needle marks on arms/legs (from intravenous use)
    • Constricted pupils
    • Engaging in risky activities
    • Confusion
    • Flushed and/or itchy skin
    • Slow, sluggish movements
    • Nausea
    • Falling asleep at inappropriate or unusual times
    • Poor coordination
    • Neglecting responsibilities at school, work, and home
    • Dramatic mood swings
    • Aggression
    • Frequent conflicts with family and friends

    What are the signs of alcohol abuse in teens?

    The signs and symptoms of alcohol use in teens will vary greatly based on their genetic makeup and whether or not they are using drugs. However, common signs include:

    • Smell of alcohol on breath or clothes
    • Glazed or bloodshot eyes
    • Flushed skin
    • Slurring
    • Alcohol use in spite of consequences
    • Sleep disturbances
    • Poor coordination
    • Mood swings

    Can teens benefit from early intervention?

    Absolutely. Many teenagers (and adults) will try to convince their parents or caregivers that they can stop abusing substances or alcohol without any help, but research shows this is unlikely. Professional substance abuse treatment is their best bet for safely detoxing and learning healthier coping mechanisms.
